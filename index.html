01 · The starting point

B2B deals are long. We invest accordingly.

Our marketing is built around a simple truth: the work that creates a deal and the moment it closes are separated by months. Investing in that window - consistently, across markets - is how we make sure Moxso is already familiar when a CISO's buying cycle begins.

How the buying cycle works

B2B deals take shape over months, across multiple stakeholders, long before sales is involved. The marketing activity that creates a deal and the moment it closes are typically separated by six to twelve months. That is the window this investment is designed to own.

03 · What we are building

Category ownership in a market that doesn't have a clear leader yet.

Human Risk Intelligence is an emerging category. No incumbent owns it. The companies that define a category early - by publishing the framework, establishing the vocabulary, becoming the reference point - tend to hold that position for a long time.

How category creation compounds

CrowdStrike defined endpoint detection. Snyk defined developer security. Wiz defined cloud security posture. The company that published the category framework first became the reference point every buyer started from.

This is what our LinkedIn investment is building toward - a position where Moxso is the name buyers think of first when Human Risk Intelligence becomes a budget line.
The market we're leaving
Security awareness training. Crowded, commoditised, evaluated on completion rates and phishing simulations.
The category we're defining
Human Risk Intelligence. A continuous adaptive system - and a category with no established leader.
We have approximately 18 months to establish that position before the window closes.
