01 · The starting point

The question we're anticipating.

17,326.64 EUR/month on average in LinkedIn ads. A four-person team. Where's the direct line to revenue? It's a fair question. This isn't a defence — it's an argument that it's the wrong question, and that asking the right one changes everything.

The measurement problem

B2B deals don't follow monthly reporting cycles. They take shape over months, across multiple stakeholders, long before sales is involved. Measuring marketing in 30-day windows misses most of what creates a deal — and credits whatever was visible at the finish line.

Not a Moxso position. What the data shows across millions of B2B customer journeys.

The question isn't "what did we get from LinkedIn this month?"

It's "what did last year's work build for this quarter?"

By the time a CISO books a demo, they have already formed a view — shaped by content they read, peers they spoke to, and brands they kept encountering. The sales conversation is not where the decision begins. It is where it finishes.

The practical consequence

A CISO who has seen Moxso in their LinkedIn feed for six months responds to outreach differently than one who has never heard of us. Same message, different conversion rate. The difference is not the sales motion — it is what happened upstream.

That upstream work doesn't appear in this month's pipeline. It appears in win rates, deal velocity, and the quality of conversations sales is having.

Most B2B buyers don't choose the best product.

They choose the one they've seen the most.

The structural problem most companies miss

Familiarity doesn't happen by accident. It requires systematic, sustained presence at the account level — over months, not campaigns. Most companies can't do this because their entire go-to-market is wired for demand capture: search ads, retargeting, SDR sequences. Infrastructure built to harvest intent, not build it.

When outbound fails, the instinct is to fix the sequence. The real fix is upstream — building the familiarity that makes outbound land.

04 · What we are actually building

Not a lead generation programme. A category ownership position.

Security awareness training is a crowded, commoditised market. Competing on feature terms is a race to the bottom that incumbents with ten-year head starts will win. Moxso's position is different.

How category creation compounds

CrowdStrike defined endpoint detection. Snyk defined developer security. Wiz defined cloud security posture. The company that published the category framework first became the reference point every buyer started from.

Our marketing investment is category infrastructure spend. Done consistently, it compounds into a structural advantage that is genuinely hard to replicate.
